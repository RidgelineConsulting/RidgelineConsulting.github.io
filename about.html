My Approach

The diagnosis is the work.

Most consultants take the client's stated problem at face value and start building a solution for the symptom. I don't work that way.

Before I build anything, I need to understand what's actually happening. Not what someone told me in the first meeting. Not what the job posting says. Not the version that sounds clean enough for a boardroom. The real version.

I research the company before we ever talk. I ask where it hurts. I identify who owns the problem, not just who reported it. I watch for the things people don't say out loud. Then I benchmark what's working, what's broken, and what's missing entirely.

Only after that diagnosis is done do I start building. And when I build, it's for production use. Real tools, real frameworks, real systems that your team picks up and runs with on Monday morning.

Thirteen years in defense contracting taught me to read the gap between what the spec says and what's actually true. That's the skill I bring to every engagement.
